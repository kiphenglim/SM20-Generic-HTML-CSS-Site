BLM and Corporations

Let me be clear: I am not black, nor do I presume to speak over black people. But these issues have been raised by black individuals for a long time, and as a person with privilege, it is my duty to amplify their voices.

The last week has been exceedingly stressful for many people of colour. For people of colour, the ongoing reality of police brutality has overflowed into anger, sadness, and grief.

The black community is quite familiar with the usual PR tactics that many companies engage in for nothing more than superficial optics. Queer people have long commented on the fact that companies often change their logos to rainbow or sell pride merch, profiting off people's identities without making any institutional changes that actually support members of the community. They are mostly silent this year due to the absence of Pride and a more urgent issue affecting the black community. Instead, companies have been posting statements supporting black people and posting black squares rather than advocating or amplifying the needs of black individuals.

I cannot speak for other industries, but the tech sector is severely lacking in minority representation. The majority of employees are White or Asian males, even at the largest and most resource rich companies. A quick glance at Google's 2020 Diversity Annual Report shows:

Race/Ethnicity (US) % Representation in Workforce
Asian 41.9%
Black 3.7%
Latinx 5.9%
Native American 0.8%
White 51.7%

Leadership statistics are even worse. To Google's credit, they are making their statistics easily accessible on the web. Plus, Google is only the industry end of a long education pipeline that has been failing its Black and Latinx students for a very long time. The trends of representation at Google are echoed throughout many a university's software engineering program and especially in College Board's AP Computer Science exams.

Even so, it's not enough to post a #blackouttuesday square on Instagram or send a bland statement email to users. Companies should be following through on their words: financially support black-run organisations, step up hiring of people of colour, and provide resources to black employees.

Signing off for now.

-Kip, 03 June 2020
